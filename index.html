Three years ago…


The scene is set. A PTA curry and quiz night in a school hall in Battle. A bearded man waits in nervous anticipation for the hot young languages teacher from room 53 to join the team—his first chance at something more than the fleeting exchange of small-talk while he refills the printer, or turns her computer off and on again.


Why is she late?


Ten minutes later, the team is complete and the quiz is in full-flow. They laugh together, share anecdotes: she gives him a beer—he is in love. He realises his second-guessing of the team’s perfectly reasonable answers has caused more harm than good. She finds this endearing.


Is there something there between them?


As they put chairs and tables away at the end of the evening, he asks if he could add her on Facebook. She chuckles, “of course you can.”

Five whole months later…


Why has it taken this long to find out he's single? Why did nobody tell her?


Thanks Barnes!


Taking out her frustration on the pastry she's making in her tiny house in Battle, she finds herself unable to keep her thoughts from wandering to the upcoming pub quiz – or, more to the point, to the thought of that sexy man joining her on the team.


Still a whole week away though; basically an eternity. Why did she not ask him for his number? … but he IS on Facebook!


Unable to contain herself any longer, she opens her laptop and scrolls to find Lee’s profile. Keyboard now covered in pastry, she types the message: “HAPPY WEEKEND!” and clicks send.


Ten o’clock that night, while sharing a pint at Chequers, both of them realise this friendly meet-up had become somewhat of an unofficial date and that they were destined to be more than just work colleagues or friends.
